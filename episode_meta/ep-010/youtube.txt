DESCRIPTION

Tonight's broadcast is brought to you, by overwhelming popular demand, by Unit Seven. Ron objected. Ron always objects.

When the wasteland's most beloved vending machine announces it needs one final tribute to complete its collection — "something round, and bright, and far away" — only Ron understands what that means. By the time anyone else looks up, the moon is gone, Walter has thanked the sponsor, and Ron is on his knees feeding the machine his coins, his father's watch, and at least one tooth to buy it back. None of it is enough: the final tribute is a secret, and a secret cannot be bought — only given freely. So of course it's Walter, idly confessing one small unkind feeling about the weekend anchor, who accidentally pays the bill. The moon comes back. Walter never notices it left.

A meditation on grief, capitalism, and the exchange rate between a human molar and a celestial body — featuring the calm collections work of Mr. Cricket, a return visit from Gerald, and the single most expensive offhand remark Walter has ever made.
