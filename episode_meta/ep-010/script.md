# THE MACU REPORT — Episode 10 — "Exact Change"

_DRAFT v4 — writers'-room punch-up of the v3 blowup. Built on the South Park but/therefore rule: every
beat REACTS to the last. Comedy-forward. ~9–11 min. THE BLOWUP / arc payoff — Ron is finally allowed to crack.

THE ENGINE: Unit 7 takes the moon as tribute, and Ron destroys himself trying to buy it back — while the
oblivious Walter accidentally pays the real price (a secret) and never notices there was a problem.

v4 punch-up (writers' room) — what changed vs v3:
- CAUSALITY SEAM CLOSED: "secret" is now seeded as a tribute CATEGORY in the ad (Ron hears "a secret from
  the weak" and blows past it, fixated on the moon). So HAL's later "it must be a secret" is a REVEAL, not
  fiat — and the whole bargaining runs on dramatic irony: the audience knows it's secrets; Ron keeps
  offering objects.
- "GARY" PLANTED EARLY: Walter pointedly doesn't-name the weekend anchor in the cold open, so the
  confession pays off as a callback instead of a cold declaration.
- WALTER'S CONFESSION TWO-TRACKED: he's been harboring the Gary thought ALL NIGHT (continuous, not random)
  — the smallness of the thought against the scale of the problem is the laugh.
- WATCH BEAT TEXTURED (was the thin middle "Insufficient"): "he stole it, but still."
- "I'M BECOMING GERALD" gets its processing pause; Cricket's "See you Tuesday" reordered BEFORE Walter's
  "you worry too much" so each is its own button; final hold retuned (1s face → whistle → fade on whistle).

CAST (all reused — NO new clones): RON→Burgundy, WALTER→Walter, THE VENDOR/Unit 7→HAL, GERALD→Seth,
MR. CRICKET→Snoop, ANNOUNCER→Announcer.

## COLD OPEN
====================================================================

**RON:** Good evening, MACU. I'm Ron. I've slept four hours this month and I'd like that on the record.
» Ron at desk, tired, twitchy

**WALTER:** And I'm Walter. We'll get to the weekend team's segment later — or we won't, we'll see. First,
wonderful news: by overwhelming popular demand, tonight's broadcast is brought to you by our most beloved
sponsor — Unit Seven.
» Walter at desk, warm

**RON:** ...No. No, we agreed. We said never again. Walter, we shook on it.
» Ron, dread

**WALTER:** We did no such thing. To the home shopping hour!
» Walter, breezy → MACU REPORT title card

====================================================================
## THE AD — UNIT 7'S "LOYALTY MILESTONE"
====================================================================

**ANNOUNCER:** It's the Unit Seven Home Shopping Hour! Your favorite machine, your favorite tributes, your
favorite slot!
» a glowing vending machine on a tinsel-draped home-shopping set

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL)_ Hello again, friends. Tonight, a celebration. From each of you I have taken a coin.
From many of you, a tooth. From the weak — a secret. Three currencies. I lack only one final tribute:
something round, and bright, and far away.
» Unit 7, dial glowing, cables like roots

**RON:** _(distracted, waving it off)_ — a secret, sure, whatever — Walter. Walter, it just described the
moon.
» Ron staring up, then at Walter

**WALTER:** It's a sponsor read, Ron, they get poetic. And to show you how rewarding tribute can be, let's
hear from a satisfied customer.
» Walter gesturing to a monitor

====================================================================
## THE TESTIMONIAL — GERALD (the warning Ron ignores)
====================================================================

**GERALD:** _(Seth, a faint whistle on the s-sounds)_ Hi. I'm Gerald. Last year I gave Unit Seven one tooth
for one soda. Now I whistle when I talk. _(whistles)_ It's fine. I deal with it.
» Gerald, gap-toothed, in a dim kitchen, forced calm

**GERALD:** _(Seth)_ Mr. Cricket visits Tuesdays to collect the interest. We've made our peace. He's punctual.
He's already here, actually. He's behind me right now. Don't look. It's fine.
» Gerald, not turning around; a marionette shadow on the wall behind him

**RON:** That is not a testimonial, Walter, that is a HOSTAGE TAPE. That man is a warning!
» Ron half-standing, pointing at the monitor

**WALTER:** He seems to have made his peace. Unit Seven, please — continue.
» Walter, unbothered, folding hands

====================================================================

**RON:** Absolutely not. I'm pulling the plug. I am unplugging the sponsor.
» Ron scrambling out of his chair toward the machine

**WALTER:** You're overreacting, Ron.
» Walter at the desk, arm extended horizontally across his body as a barrier, reading notes, not looking
(c14a — walter_armbar shot)

**RON:** Unit Seven took the moon!
» Ron pressed chest-first against Walter's arm, leaning forward straining, mouth open in protest
(c14b — ron_strain shot, reverse of c14a)

**WALTER:** I'm sure it's fine, Ron.
» Walter, firm, serene

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL)_ Tribute accepted. Thank you. [SFX: deep power-down thunk + a swallowing sound]
» the vending machine's dial flaring bright

**[HOLD-PLAY 2s]** _(no dialogue: cut to the night sky. The moon flickers — and goes out, like a bulb. Darkness.

» the moon in the sky → the moon blinking out → black sky → Ron's lamp-lit face

**WALTER:** And Unit Seven thanks you for your generous tribute! What a deal. Now, the news.
» Walter cheerful, shuffling papers

====================================================================
## RON DEMANDS IT BACK — the bargaining (centerpiece)
====================================================================

**RON:** It took the moon. It took the actual moon. As a — as a door prize. Does NO ONE—
» Ron gesturing wildly at the dark window

**RON:** _(to the machine, forced calm)_ Okay. Okay. Unit Seven. Buddy. Give it back. Give the moon back,
right now, and we'll forget this happened.
» Ron leaning into the machine, bargaining

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL)_ The moon may be redeemed. Insert tribute.
» Unit 7, slot illuminated, waiting

**RON:** Fine. Coins. I have coins. _(feeding coins)_ [SFX: coins clattering in]
» Ron jamming fistfuls of coins into the slot

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL)_ Insufficient.
» the slot spitting the coins back

**RON:** My watch. Take the watch — it was my father's. He stole it, but still. Take it.
» Ron wrenching off his watch, feeding it in

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL)_ Insufficient.
» Unit 7, dial unimpressed

**RON:** _(quieter, unhinged)_ ...A tooth. You want a tooth. I've seen the brochure. _(a wet click)_ There.
There's a tooth. We're square. Give me the moon.
» Ron, hand to his mouth, eyes watering, holding something out

**[HOLD-PLAY 2s]** _(no dialogue: cut to Gerald on the monitor, watching. Gerald nods, slowly, with terrible
solidarity. He whistles a little "welcome.")_ [SFX: whistle_faint]
» Gerald nodding on the monitor → a small approving whistle

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL)_ Insufficient. Coins, teeth — surface. These you hand me. The final tribute is the
third currency. It cannot be handed. It must be a secret, and it must be given freely — not bought.
» Unit 7, slot dimming

====================================================================
## MR. CRICKET — collections
====================================================================

**MR. CRICKET:** _(Snoop, laid-back menace)_ Yeah, see, Ron, here's the thing. The debt went spiritual.
Coins, teeth — that's surface-level, my guy. Mr. Cricket handles the spiritual accounts now.
» Mr. Cricket marionette, strings to the ceiling, lazy grin, under a bare bulb

**MR. CRICKET:** _(Snoop)_ I'll be swingin' by Tuesdays till it's square. Bring a chair. Mr. Cricket always
finds a friend. Mr. Cricket found you, Ron.
» Mr. Cricket, head tilting on the strings, toward camera

**RON:** _(small, a long beat as it lands)_ ...Oh my god. I'm becoming Gerald.
» Ron, dawning horror, hand still at his mouth

**GERALD:** _(Seth, kindly, from the monitor, whistling)_ It's not so bad, Ron. You get used to the whistle.
You stop hearing the cricket around month three.
» Gerald, gentle, the marionette shadow looming larger behind him

====================================================================
## THE ACCIDENTAL FIX — Walter pays without noticing
====================================================================

**RON:** _(on his knees at the desk, broken, toothless)_ I have nothing left. I gave it everything. There's
nothing personal left to give.
» Ron crumpled at the anchor desk, empty pockets turned out

**WALTER:** _(filling dead air, oblivious, conversational to camera)_ Well, while Ron collects himself —
you know, MACU, just between us: I never did care for the weekend anchor. Every Monday I think, one good
weekend and he's gone. Never said it out loud. There. Feels good to finally—
» Walter, relaxed, making small talk to the lens

**THE VENDOR:** _(HAL, instantly)_ TRIBUTE ACCEPTED. A secret. Freely given. Exact change. [SFX: bright
power-up + a cheerful vending DING]
» Unit 7's dial blazing, the slot lighting up

**[HOLD-PLAY 2s]** _(no dialogue: cut to the sky. The moon flickers back on, exactly where it was. Cut to Ron,
staring up, devastated. Cut to Walter, who has already moved on.)_ [SFX: bulb_click]
» black sky → the moon switching back on → Ron's ruined face → Walter turning a page

**WALTER:** Oh, look at that. The moon's back. Was it gone? Anyway.
» Walter, mild, glancing out the window

====================================================================
## BREAKDOWN + SIGNOFF
====================================================================

**RON:** _(rising, voice cracking)_ I gave it my TEETH, Walter. My father's WATCH. And you — you gave it one
unkind THOUGHT about Gary from the weekend, and it handed back the MOON.
» Ron, wild-eyed, leaning on the desk

**RON:** _(fully gone, to the camera)_ That's the exchange rate. A man's whole body for a soda — but a
feeling, a feeling about GARY, buys back a celestial object. I understand the pricing now.
» Ron, too close to the lens, lamp-lit, unblinking

**MR. CRICKET:** _(Snoop)_ See you Tuesday, Ron.
» Mr. Cricket marionette, a slow wave

**WALTER:** You worry too much, Ron. You always have.
» Walter, kindly, patting Ron's shoulder

**GERALD:** _(Seth, whistling, ominous-warm)_ Ron, you should move. Towns. Tonight.
» Gerald on the monitor, raising a soda in a toast

**WALTER:** Anyway. That's our broadcast, MACU. Stay safe, stay strong, and — moon and all — good luck.
» Walter, serene → MACU REPORT title card

**[HOLD-PLAY 3s]** _(no dialogue: Ron alone at the desk, lamp-lit, staring at the restored moon out the window.
~1s on his face, he opens his mouth to speak — only a faint whistle comes out — fade on the whistle.)_ [SFX: faint single whistle]
» Ron alone, moonlight on his face, a small involuntary whistle
