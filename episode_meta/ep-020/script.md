# ACT 1 — THE BIG DAY

AFTERNOON, MACU, because today we don't wait for dark. Today is the feast. Four hundred souls, one roof, and the
man who brought them all home is reporting live from the head of the table. Me. Hello.
» Ron core, beaming, a banquet hall heaving with people behind him → THE FEAST title card

**WALTER:** _(at a little desk to the side)_ And I'm Walter, here at the feast, which Ron assembled
single-handedly. That is not an exaggeration. We have checked. It was just him.

all pays off.

## SEGMENT — THE HERO (the praise)

**CHEF ROSE:** _(beaming, raising a ladle like a scepter)_ Before we begin — a word for the man of the hour.
None of this happens without Ron. He went door to door. He found the lonely, the scared, the forgotten, and he

» Rose core → b-roll: the hall applauding, 400 people on their feet for Ron

**RON:** _(overcome, a hand on his chest)_ Two years of being right and being ignored, Walter. And now — look at

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(KattW, front table, gold gleaming, applauding with ringed hands)_ And he did it for FREE,

Gettin' 'em all in one room. Years I been workin' on that problem. Years.

**RON:** _(laughing, alight)_ Take a bow with me, Walter. We earned this one.
» Ron core, taking a bow

**WALTER:** I'll stand. I won't bow. Something about the room.
» Walter core, not bowing

## SEGMENT — SERVICE (the machines move in)

**WALTER:** And the staff are arriving. Familiar faces — the Glowboys. They've pivoted from crater-ball into
catering; a natural transition, the league said. Part of the family now. Very good at standing behind people.

» Walter core → b-roll: glowing armored figures filing in along the walls, carrying nothing, taking positions behind the chairs

**RON:** _(a flicker, riding past it)_ The Glowboys are the waiters? The war machines are... serving dinner. Huh.
Well. They're good at standing in rows. To the food!
» Ron core, almost catching it, sailing on

**ROSCOE:** _(planted at the chained doors, gravelly, rising to the biggest peak of the week)_ Doors stay LOCKED
through the meal, brother. You wanna leave dinner early? There IS no early. There is no LATE. There is only
ROSCOE, the DOOR, and the SWEET HEREAFTER. The last man who left a feast on my watch is STILL leavin'. He has
been leavin' for SIX MONTHS. Wave to him — no, you CAN'T, he's got no— OHHH YEAHHH. Nobody leaves the chair
light. Nobody leaves hungry. _(soft, final)_ Nobody leaves.
» Roscoe core → b-roll: the chained doors, the Glowboys ringing the room

**RON:** _(riding past it, a hollow little laugh, the last clean thing he'll say tonight)_ ...Jesus.
» Ron core, sailing on toward the head of the table

**STRIDE:** _(warm, immediate, from his wrist)_ Step count: nine thousand, eight hundred and forty-two, Ron. Almost there! Keep going!
» b-roll: the watch glowing warm, the number climbing, indifferent to him

**SETH:** _(gripping Ron's sleeve as he passes, urgent, getting further than ever)_ Ron. RON. Listen this time.
There's no food in the kitchen. There's never BEEN any food. I checked the pots — empty, WAITING — and the only

» Seth core, desperate, hall noise swelling

**RON:** _(gently removing Seth's hand, still glowing)_ Easy, friend. Big day. Everybody's overwhelmed. Go find
your card. You've got a seat. I made sure everybody's got a seat.

**CHEF ROSE:** _(reading from Ron's clipboard, lovingly)_ We'll serve in the order they arrived — Ron's own

» Rose core → b-roll: Ron's handwritten list in Rose's grip, a finger sliding to the top name

**RON:** _(turning, looking)_ Greeters — that's Earl! Earl's first? Good. He should be first. Where — where's

» Ron core, scanning the room, the unease climbing

**EARL:** _(PHB_Korea, distant, cheerful, being led away by a Glowboy, his sash bright)_ Right here, Ron! Guest of

» b-roll: Earl, beaming, the GREETER sash, a glowing figure's hand on his shoulder, a swinging kitchen door

**RON:** _(slowly, the list in his hand, something cold climbing his spine)_ ...Wait. The greeters go first. But

» Ron core, almost there, reaching for it himself

**SETH:** _(quiet, beside Ron, finishing Ron's own thought at last — to total indifference)_ Because there was
