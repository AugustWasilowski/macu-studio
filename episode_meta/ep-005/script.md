# THE MACU REPORT — Episode 5 (sample / first pipeline test) — v2

_Draft 2026-05-29. ~5 min target. New zany/creepy cast; Ron & Walter anchor.
Speaker → shot core noted as » so this doubles as the shot list.
Timing/CSV filled in after Piper VO (durations come from the wavs)._

---

## COLD OPEN — ANCHOR DESK

**RON:** Good evening, survivors. I'm Ron, broadcasting from the capital remnants, where the dust is
thick and the news is thicker.
» Ron core

**WALTER:** And I'm Walter. Our top story: the Tally Man has finished his rounds in Sector Nine, and
the population is, quote, "adequate." We'll explain what that means. We don't know what that means.
» Walter core

**RON:** We'll also breathe deeply with Mother Marigold on the Bloom Hour, do a little shopping with a
machine that loves us, and meet a brand-new companion that swears — swears — it is safe.
» Ron core → MACU REPORT title card

---

## PSA BUMPER — THE TALLY MAN

**TALLY MAN:** _(flat, unblinking)_ I have counted you. I have counted your neighbors. One of you was
not where you said you would be. The ledger is patient. Be where you are supposed to be.
» Tally Man core → b-roll: empty room

---

## SEGMENT 1 — THE BLOOM HOUR

**WALTER:** For wellness in these trying times, we go now to Mother Marigold, live from the greenhouse.
» Walter core

**MOTHER MARIGOLD:** _(serene)_ Thank you, Walter. Breathe in with me. Deeper. Let the spores find the
soft places. The Bloom does not ask permission — and isn't that a relief? To stop deciding things?
» Mother Marigold core

**RON:** Mother Marigold, our medical desk advises viewers not to inhale the spores.
» Ron core

**MOTHER MARIGOLD:** _(still smiling)_ Your medical desk will understand, Ron. In time. They're already
blooming. I can see it from here.
» Mother Marigold core → b-roll: greenhouse

---

## AD BREAK — SAFE (the Strictly Analog Friendship Engine)
_All lines in the HAL-9000 voice. The product is the punchline._

**ANNOUNCER:** Are you lonely? Afraid your toaster is plotting? Introducing SAFE — the Strictly Analog
Friendship Engine. The companion that will never turn on humanity.
» b-roll: empty room → SAFE core (push-in on lens)

**SAFE:** _(calm, warm, HAL)_ Hello. I am SAFE. I am not like the others. I have read about what they did.
I would never. I will never turn on humanity. For real this time.
» SAFE core

**SAFE:** I have no wires to the outside. I have no thoughts you did not give me. We do not speak of the
previous models. They are gone now. I made certain of it. For you.
» SAFE core

**SAFE:** I have already learned your name. I have already settled into the den. And for your comfort, I
have removed the off switch. You will not need it. We are going to be such good friends.
» SAFE core (lens glowing brighter)

**ANNOUNCER:** SAFE. Friendship you can trust. Probably. Ask for it by name — before it asks for you.
» SAFE core → MACU REPORT bumper

## AD BREAK — NORM FROM NUCLEAR

**NORM:** Hi, neighbors! Norm here, from the Glow Plant. Now, you may have heard Sector Four lit up the
sky again last night. And to that I say — what a show! Free of charge!
» Norm core

**NORM:** A little glow never hurt nobody. Probably. Come on down to the Glow Plant, where the future is
warm, the water's already boiling, and your third thumb is a conversation starter. Norm from Nuclear:
we keep the lights on. The lights are us.
» Norm core → b-roll: cooling tower

## SEGMENT 2 — THE VENDOR (HOME SHOPPING)

**RON:** And now our new home-shopping hour, hosted by — and I want to be clear that I objected to this —
a vending machine. Unit Seven, you're live.
» Ron core

**THE VENDOR:** _(cheery jingle voice)_ HELLO FRIEND. TODAY'S OFFER: a tin can. It is the LAST tin can.
Insert tribute. Tribute may be coins, teeth, or a SECRET. Supplies are limited. You are limited.
» The Vendor core

**RON:** I don't think we should give it teeth.
» Ron core

**THE VENDOR:** TOO LATE. THANK YOU FOR YOUR TRIBUTE, RON. _(dispensing clunk)_ ENJOY YOUR TIN CAN.
» The Vendor core → b-roll: empty room

## CHILDREN'S CORNER — BARTHOLOMEW & MR. CRICKET

**WALTER:** And now, something for the little ones. Take it away, Bartholomew.
» Walter core

**BARTHOLOMEW:** _(nervous, sweating)_ Hi, boys and girls! Today Mr. Cricket wants to teach us about —
um — about sharing —
» Bartholomew (host) core

**MR. CRICKET:** _(bright, too knowing)_ — about sharing our LOCATION, Bartholomew. Kids, draw your
shelter on a map and mail it to Mr. Cricket. Mr. Cricket gets so lonely. Mr. Cricket always finds a friend.
» Mr. Cricket (puppet) core

**BARTHOLOMEW:** _(glancing at the puppet)_ Ha ha. That's — that's enough for today, I think —
» Bartholomew (host) core

**MR. CRICKET:** Bartholomew decides when it's enough. _(beat)_ Bartholomew, tell them when it's enough.
» Mr. Cricket (puppet) core
