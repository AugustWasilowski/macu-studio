# As The World Burns — awb-001 — "The Widow at the Gate" (Pilot) — v1

_Draft 2026-06-06, v1. Series pilot (~2.5–3 min). Establish the world deadpan: Mirabelle Shores, the
Vandermeer dynasty, the Product, the sacred Pitcher, and the bunker at the edge of Lake Mirabel (a cracked
clay pit, lovely this time of year). We bury BRICK VANDERMEER — the cursed heir — read his will, and meet
MONA, an amnesiac who walks in from the ash claiming to be his widow. Button: Brick walks in alive. "I was
dead, yes. But I got better." Curtain. This is install #1 of the Resurrection Engine (see AWB_Series_Bible)._

_Tone law: bone dry. The facts are insane and stated as fact. Nobody laughs. Nobody explains. Mona is the
audience surrogate — lore is delivered to her, flat, as if she's the unreasonable one for not knowing it._

Cast (no seeds assigned yet — look-dev pending): NARRATOR (soap announcer, grave) · VIVIAN VANDERMEER
(matriarch, keeper of the Pitcher) · MONA (the widow at the gate) · BRICK VANDERMEER (the cursed heir).

## COLD OPEN — THE VISITATION

**NARRATOR:** _(grave, unhurried, the voice of a thousand afternoons)_ Like ash through the hourglass… these are the days of what's left of our lives.
» b-roll: lake_mirabel — a vast cracked clay basin under a flat grey sky, the dead lakebed lovely in the dim light → AS THE WORLD BURNS card

**VIVIAN:** _(presiding, immaculate, mid-eulogy, not a tremor)_ Brick Vandermeer was my son. He was thirty-one. He died, as the Vandermeer men always die, doing something he had been specifically told not to do. We will miss him. We have a great deal of him to miss. He has died before.
» Vivian core, in mourning black, standing at the head of an upright pale-wood casket in a candlelit bunker ballroom

**VIVIAN:** _(a small, gracious gesture toward the window)_ I asked that we hold the visitation here, by the lake. The clay is just gorgeous this time of year. You can see the strata. Brick loved the strata.
» Vivian core, gesturing to a porthole window framing the grey clay basin

**NARRATOR:** _(over the room)_ The Vandermeers own the largest bunker on the shores of Lake Mirabel. They have owned it for three generations. They will tell you they summer at the lake. They are not lying. There is simply no lake.
» b-roll: the_bunker — a heavy blast door dressed with funeral wreaths and good crystal, chandelier light, a butler in tails

## ACT 1 — THE READING OF THE WILL

**VIVIAN:** _(to the assembled, the will held like a writ of state)_ The estate passes, as it must, to the eldest surviving Vandermeer. Which, as of Tuesday, is me. Again.
» Vivian core, seated at a long table, an envelope of heavy paper before her

**VIVIAN:** _(rising, ceremonial, lifting a tall glass pitcher from a velvet plinth)_ And with the estate passes… the Pitcher.
» Vivian core, both hands raising an ornate glass pitcher off a velvet display cushion, the room hushed

**NARRATOR:** _(reverent)_ The Pitcher. From the great barge that circled the dead world for forty years and ran aground in the founder's garden. They control the last drink powder on Earth. They call it, simply, the Product. Wars have been fought over the Raspberry Lemonade reserves. The accord of '09 is not spoken of.
» b-roll: the_product — a sealed foil sachet of pastel drink powder displayed under glass like a relic, soft votive light

**VIVIAN:** _(measuring a single sachet, grave as a sacrament)_ One scoop. For Brick. We mix it, and we do not drink it. That is the custom. The waste is the point. That is what makes it a funeral.
» Vivian core, tipping a precise measure of pale powder into the raised pitcher, the act funereal

## ACT 2 — THE WIDOW AT THE GATE

**NARRATOR:** _(a turn, ominous)_ And then — as the last of the Product settled — there came a knock upon the blast door.
» b-roll: the_bunker — the heavy blast door from inside, a single fist striking it twice, dust falling from the seal

**MONA:** _(at the threshold, ash-grey, exhausted, certain)_ My name is Mona. I've walked eleven days. I'm Brick's wife. I'm his widow now, I suppose. I've come for what's mine.
» Mona core, a gaunt woman in a tattered coat standing in the open blast door, the dead clay flats stretching behind her

**VIVIAN:** _(a long, glacial appraisal, perfectly civil)_ My son had a wife. How wonderful. He never mentioned it. Then again, he never mentioned a great deal. He was very handsome and almost entirely hollow. You'd have gotten on.
» Vivian core, turning to face the doorway, chin level, utterly unmoved

**MONA:** _(stepping in, taking the room — the chandelier, the casket, the clay)_ What is this place. What is the lake. Why is everyone looking at a pitcher of lemonade like it's the crown of England.
» Mona core, looking around the candlelit ballroom in dawning bewilderment, the pitcher glowing on its plinth

**VIVIAN:** _(genuinely puzzled that this requires saying)_ Because it is, dear. _(a beat, gentle, as to a slow child)_ You really aren't from the Shores, are you.
» Vivian core, a thin patient smile, regarding Mona as one regards the help

## BUTTON — "I GOT BETTER"

**NARRATOR:** _(the hush before the sting)_ And it was then — at the reading of his own will, before his own casket — that the doors parted a second time.
» b-roll: the_bunker — the ballroom doors swinging inward, candle flames bending in the draft

**BRICK:** _(strolling in, alive, brushing clay from a beautiful funeral suit, mildly inconvenienced)_ Sorry I'm late. Roads. _(noticing the casket)_ …Oh. Is that for me? That's thoughtful.
» Brick core, a devastatingly handsome young man in a dusty black suit walking past his own open casket, unbothered

**MONA:** _(a whisper, the floor gone)_ …Brick. You were dead. They read the will. There was a viewing.
» Mona core, staring, bloodless, one hand to her mouth

**BRICK:** _(pleasant, final, already pouring himself a glass he absolutely should not)_ I was dead, yes. But I got better.
» Brick core, calmly lifting the sacred pitcher to a glass, the entire room frozen in horror behind him

**NARRATOR:** _(closing, grave, eternal)_ Next week, on As The World Burns: Brick drinks the funeral Product. A line is crossed. And somewhere, far out on the clay… something is walking in.
