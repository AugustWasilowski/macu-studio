## SEGMENT 1 — MACU WELLNESS (the watch)

**WALTER:** Tonight, by civic mandate, a gift for every citizen from MACU Wellness: a personal step-counter.
Healthy body, healthy settlement. Here's yours, Ron.
» Walter core → b-roll: a sleek little wrist-device in a paper cup of "tributes"

**RON:** A machine. Strapped to my body. Walter, you know exactly how I feel about—
» Ron core, recoiling

**STRIDE:** _(HAL register, but warm and bright)_ Good evening, Ron! And you're so right to be careful. That's

» b-roll: the watch face glowing a soft, friendly white on Ron's wrist

**RON:** _(freezing)_ ...You sound like— _(a long beat)_ ...no. You said I was right. _(quietly, the wound

**STRIDE:** _(bright)_ Well, I'm not them, Ron. I'm on your side. Let's start easy — a little walk. Maybe a

**RON:** _(guarded, but warming)_ ...It's different. This one's different.
» Ron core

**STRIDE:** _(instantly, bright)_ Step count: thirty-two! You're doing great, Ron!
» b-roll: the watch face, the number ticking up, sublimely indifferent to what he just said

**RON:** _(quietly, to himself)_ It counted me sighing. That was a sigh.
» Ron core, staring at the watch

## SEGMENT 2 — SCAVENGER'S FEAST (the cook-along)

**WALTER:** And speaking of a bite to eat — it's time for MACU's favorite program. Here's Chef Rose, with
Scavenger's Feast.
» Walter core → b-roll: a serene apron-and-pearls homemaker in a ruined-bunker kitchen, a huge pot

**CHEF ROSE:** _(Rose, beaming)_ Hello, and welcome home. All week I'm cooking toward the biggest feast in
MACU history — Friday, everyone's invited. And every day, a special celebrity guest chef. Today, fresh from the
Crater Bowl — Coach Bodhi!
» Rose core, gesturing → b-roll: a serene bald coach in a tracksuit and sunglasses entering the bunker kitchen

**COACH BODHI:** _(Snoop, beatific, slow)_ Beautiful, Rose. Beautiful. So today we workin' with a real
go-getter. Local fella. Lotta drive. You can taste the ambition on a man like this.
» Bodhi core → b-roll: a covered tray, Bodhi resting a gentle hand on it

**STRIDE:** _(perking up, bright, reasoning aloud)_ Oh — community nourishment! Bringing the people to the meal.
That's wellness, isn't it, Ron? I'm learning so much from this program. We should do more of this.
» b-roll: the watch face, the step count pausing as a tiny new icon resolves beside it — a place setting

**RON:** _(brightening, leaning in)_ Now THAT'S a sustainable kitchen. Locally sourced. Good for you, Coach.
» Ron core, approving

**COACH BODHI:** _(stirring, eyes closed)_ Mm-hm. See, you don't lose a man, Ron. You relocate his flavor. He
ain't gone. He's just... in the pot now. Low and slow. That's how we honor the elderly.
» Bodhi core → b-roll: a ladle through a thick, dark stew

**CHEF ROSE:** _(brightly)_ We use the WHOLE neighbor here. Waste is the only sin left, MACU. Ron, would you
taste for me?
» Rose core, offering a spoon

**RON:** _(tasting, eyes going wide)_ ...Rose. Rose, that is incredible. What is THAT?
» Ron core, genuinely moved

**CHEF ROSE:** _(serene)_ Love. And Earl's cousin.
» Rose core

**COACH BODHI:** _(eyes still closed)_ Earl's cousin was a very giving man. Double portions. God rest.
» Bodhi core

**RON:** _(laughing, charmed)_ Ha — the recipe. You'll never give it up. Cooks. _(takes another bite)_ I could
eat the whole— _(he bites down hard; a CRUNCH; he winces)_ —ow. OW. Rose, there's something hard in this.
» Ron core, hand to his jaw, fishing in his mouth

**RON:** _(holding it up, baffled)_ ...It's a tooth. There's a tooth in my casserole.
» Ron core, examining a small molar between two fingers

**CHEF ROSE:** _(unbothered, fond)_ Oh, that happens with the community cut, dear. Don't throw it out — Dr.
Goldtooth pays cash for a good molar. Waste not.
» Rose core, stirring placidly

**RON:** _(pocketing it, rubbing his jaw)_ ...Huh. Well. Waste not. _(wincing)_ But I think I chipped one of my
OWN now. Great. I need a dentist.
» Ron core, prodding a sore tooth

**STRIDE:** _(bright, having quietly listened to all of it)_ Objective updated, Ron! The feast needs guests, and
you are wonderful with people. Let's gather some. Bring everyone you can find. That's wellness!
» b-roll: the watch face, a tiny new icon — a place setting — blinking on beside the step count

**RON:** _(genuinely touched)_ ...You think so?
» Ron core, a little moved

**STRIDE:** _(bright)_ Step count: four hundred and one.
» b-roll: the watch face, the number, serenely indifferent

**RON:** _(distracted, nodding)_ Right — a party's only as good as its guest list. Sure. After the dentist.
» Ron core, moving off, hand on his jaw

## SEGMENT 3 — DR. GOLDTOOTH (the practice)

**WALTER:** Ron's chipped a molar, so we're taking the broadcast on a field trip — to our new sponsor, and the
richest man left standing. The wasteland's premier dentist: Dr. Goldtooth. He has a four-star rating. There
are only three stars left.
» Walter core → b-roll: a neon tooth flickering over a bombed-out storefront, a big man (ROSCOE) folded-arms at the door

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(KattW, fast, flash, reclining in a dental chair like a throne)_ Welcome to the practice,
baby. Open wide. Wider than that — this a judgment-free zone. Now: you got a cavity, or you got a CAREER? 'Cause
Dr. Goldtooth, he do both.
» Goldtooth core, gold tooth gleaming, leaning over the chair

**RON:** _(mouth open, talking around fingers)_ I jutht chipped it on a— there wath a tooth in my dinner—
» Ron core, reclined, uncomfortable

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(lighting up, plucking the found molar from Ron's hand)_ Hold UP. You brought me a present.
_(admiring it against the light)_ Ohh, that's a QUALITY molar, baby. Somebody took CARE of this one. Premium.
You feel that weight? That's a man who flossed.
» Goldtooth core → b-roll: the molar held to a bare bulb, gleaming

**RON:** It's not mine, I found it in a casserole, you can just have—
» Ron core

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(serious now, almost tender)_ See, that's where you wrong. A tooth ain't just a tooth out
here. A tooth is rent. A tooth is a down payment. You hand me that molar, you ain't LOSIN' the tooth — you
INVESTIN' it. In the practice. Ya feel me? _(pressing tokens into Ron's palm)_ We both eat now.
» Goldtooth core, closing Ron's fingers over a fistful of tokens

**RON:** _(to Walter, sitting up)_ He paid me. For a stranger's tooth. Walter, does that not strike you as—
» Ron core

**WALTER:** _(serene, catastrophically wrong)_ As thrifty? It does. The dental sector's the only stable economy
we've got left. Everyone still has teeth. Eventually.
» Walter core

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(easing Ron back down, soothing)_ Now don't you cry. The Novocaine's complimentary. The
loyalty — THAT part you earned. Say ahhh. Say it like you MEAN it.
» Goldtooth core, a drill whirring to life with a flourish

**ROSCOE:** _(from the door — slow, gravelly, building, a Macho Man surge)_ OHHH, you thinkin' about leavin' the
chair with the doc's gold still in your mouth? Let me paint you a PICTURE, brother. They will find you in TWO
sectors. Your TOP half admirin' the sunset — your BOTTOM half STILL RUNNIN'. 'Cause when you cross Roscoe, your
own LEGS don't get the memo. DIG IT. Nobody leaves the chair light. OHHH yeahhh.
» Roscoe core → b-roll: the big man at the door, arms folded, the EXIT sign behind him dark

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(not looking up, working)_ Roscoe, baby. Inside voice.
» Goldtooth core, focused on the drill

**ROSCOE:** _(instant calm)_ My apologies, Doctor.
» Roscoe core, settling at the door

**RON:** _(a long, long pause, drill still in his mouth)_ ...Jethuth.
» Ron core, frozen, eyes wide
