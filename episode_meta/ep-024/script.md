# THE MACU Report — Episode 24 — "Sugar Free" (Season 4, Thu — The Crystal Week, ROCK BOTTOM) — v1

_Draft 2026-06-06, v1. S04-E4. THE DARK ONE. The pallet runs low — a settlement-wide supply crisis. Prices spike;
Goldtooth price-gouges his own cousin's product; Roscoe rations. RON hits bottom: the pitcher's empty, the mug is
empty, his hands shake, he's begging on the air, bargaining, doing a tremor-ridden broadcast. The "sugar-free"

at their peak: THE MUG/PITCHER (empty, shaking) · "sugar-free / scare you" (pays as the gut-punch) · STRIDE streak
breaks · "...Jesus." (Ron, about himself) · "extremely mild." Plant for ep25 (quiet recovery / the last pitcher).

## COLD OPEN — ANCHOR DESK

**RON:** _(rough, shaky, an empty mug clutched in both hands)_ Good evening, MACU. Ron here. We're — we're a little dry tonight. Supply's tight across the settlement. If anyone watching has a packet, the studio address is — Walter, what's our address. Walter. Do we have an address.
» Ron core, gaunt, sweating, gripping an empty mug → b-roll: ron_mug, empty, tilted → SUGAR FREE card

**WALTER:** _(gentle, the first crack of real concern)_ We have an address, Ron. You've given it out four times. To strangers. On the air. For lemonade. Three of those strangers came by. Two left disappointed. The third has moved in. We've put him on the couch.
» Walter core, watching him closely

**RON:** _(snapping, then deflating)_ It's not — it's not LEMONADE, Walter, it's — _(quiet)_ ...what if there's nothing in it. What if there was never anything. ...The news. Let's do the news. I need something to hold.
» Ron core, hands trembling

## SEGMENT 1 — THE CRISIS (the pallet runs dry)

**WALTER:** _(grave)_ The story tonight is scarcity. The original pallet — the one that started it all — is nearly empty. Prices have soared. A single packet now trades for a month of water rations. The settlement is, for the first time, rationing sunshine. Rationing is going smoothly. We've lost only the people we expected to, and one we didn't. We'll miss him most of all.
» b-roll: crystal_packets — the table nearly bare, one packet left under glass

**DR. GOLDTOOTH:** _(KattW, gleeful, gouging)_ Supply DOWN, demand UP — baby, that's the prettiest sentence in the LANGUAGE! Last packets goin' to AUCTION — well, all but ONE; my cousin's holdin' one back, won't tell me WHY. Family's family — but a MARKET'S a market! _(leaning in)_ I'd sell you my own GOLD TOOTH right now but I already traded it. For a packet. ...Don't tell Sweet.
» Goldtooth core, holding a single packet up to the light like a diamond

**ROSCOE:** _(PHB_IceT, rationing, Macho Man peak)_ The SUPPLY is RATIONED, brother! ONE pour per CITIZEN! You come back for seconds, you BECOME the second! The last man who double-dipped is now the REFILL! He IS the pitcher! We pour HIM at PARTIES! He's a HIT at parties, OHHH YEAHHH — but he is NO LONGER INVITED to them!
» Roscoe core, guarding the last packet on the table

**DARYL:** _(Spicoli, in full withdrawal, rattling)_ I traded my SHOES, man. For a guy's shoes, who traded ME his. We're all barefoot, trading shoes, chasing the pour. _(grabbing camera)_ Is there more. Tell me there's more. The NEWS would know. RON would know.
» Daryl core, barefoot, shaking, clutching the lens

---

## SEGMENT 2 — THE CLINIC (the sugar-free reveal)

**WALTER:** _(reading a slip of paper, mild)_ We now have lab results. The settlement clinic analyzed a pure, uncut packet of crystal. The findings: citric acid, a clouding agent, natural and artificial lemon flavor, and aspartame. No stimulant. No narcotic. No active ingredient of any kind. It is, in the clinic's words, "diet lemonade." Zero calories. Sugar-free.
» b-roll: cut_lab — a clinic slip under a single bulb, "SUGAR FREE • 0 ACTIVE INGREDIENTS"

**RON:** _(staring at the slip, the bottom dropping out)_ ...Sugar-free. There's — nothing in it. Nothing. I shook for nothing. I poured everyone's letters into a pitcher of NOTHING. _(very quiet)_ ...There's not even anything to quit.
» Ron core, holding the slip, very still

**SWEET:** _(PHB_Silky, calm, almost kind)_ I told you on Monday, Ron. It's sugar-free. That's the part that should scare you. There was never anything in the packet. The thing you couldn't put down — that was all you. _(a beat)_ Good news is: a thing that was always you, you can take back.
» Sweet core, quiet, not selling for once

**RON:** _(a long pause — the first honest breath in four days)_ ...Jesus. ...It was me.
» Ron core, the runner lands on himself, real this time

**WALTER:** _(flat, easing the cut, no joke)_ We'll let that sit. A word from the people who'd like to sit with you.
» Walter core

Unlike certain beverages, EVERWELL is a subscription you can finally stop chasing — because EVERWELL chases you. It isn't a coffin. It's a subscription. Your first month is complimentary. So was everyone's. They all rested. Reviews are unavailable — but they would have loved it. Rest now.

## WEATHER

**WALTER:** And the forecast — speaking of dry. Dry. Very dry. A high of seventy-five and not a cloud, not a drop, not a pour anywhere on the map. The settlement is advised to drink water — actual water — a sentence I did not expect to deliver this week. Condition rating: extremely mild.
» Walter core → MACU WEATHER card

## SIGNOFF

**RON:** _(hollow, but the smallest bit clearer, setting the empty mug down)_ That's our broadcast, MACU. There was never anything in the packet. _(a beat)_ I think that means I can stop. I don't feel better. But I think I can stop.
» Ron core, setting the empty mug down on the desk → MACU REPORT title card

**WALTER:** _(gentle, no joke this time)_ That's the most honest thing you've said all week, Ron. Anyway. Goodnight, MACU — stay safe, stay strong, and good luck. Drink water.
» Walter core

**(over black — no watch glow this time, just quiet)**
**RON:** _(alone, small)_ ...Sweet said I could take it back. ...I'd like to take it back.
» b-roll: the empty news desk, the empty mug, the lemonade stand dark in the corner
