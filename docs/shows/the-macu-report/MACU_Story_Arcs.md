## ARC 1 — "Ron Sees It" (Ron's paranoia vs. an oblivious society)

**Premise.** Ron is quietly distrustful and incredulous that society tolerates the dormant-AI
dangers all around it — Unit 7 / the Vendor, SAFE, sentient appliances. He's the only one who
seems to notice. Everyone else — especially Walter — is totally oblivious, calm, fine with it.

**The eventual payoff (much later — not soon).** Ron finally *cracks*. The show shifts vibe
completely and drops into his conspiracy headspace: the AIs are back, it's the AI Wars all over
again, it's happening NOW. We live inside the paranoia for a stretch. Then Walter, unbothered,
just says **"Anyway,"** — and the broadcast snaps back to normal as if none of it happened. The
tonal whiplash is the joke.

**How to seed it (per episode, from ep7 on).**
- Give Ron **1–2 small beats** of suspicion or objection that *no one acknowledges*. He flags a
  danger; the others move right past it.
- Walter stays unflappable and dismissive — the immovable straight man.
- Do **not** let Ron crack yet. Escalate the intensity gradually across episodes.

**Breakout cadence (planned 2026-05-30, revised).** Keep it on a LOW SIMMER between breakouts — 1–2 small,
ignored Ron beats per episode, no structural change. Stage the escalation like this:
- **ep8** — low simmer (done; ~1–2/10; planted the "under the static" line).
- **ep9 — looser, barely-simmering PREP.** Mostly standalone-funny; keep the in-episode joke engineering
  cranked but pull the serialization way back to ONE feather-light, totally-ignored Ron static beat. Do NOT
  spend the ep10 powder here. This is the calm-before-the-storm so the ep10 blowup hits harder.
- **ep10 — THE BLOWUP.** The "Please Stand By" broadcast-intrusion goes loud (design banked below). This is
  the big one — the intrusion is undeniable on-air, Ron is vindicated in the moment, the show genuinely
  destabilizes. Walter still "Anyway,"-s past it at the very end, but this time it costs something.
- **ep11 — POST-BLOWUP RETURN TO INCREDULOUS PARANOIA.** Aftermath/reset: the broadcast snaps back to a
  normal newscast as if ep10 never happened. Ron is the only one who remembers/believes it was real;
  everyone else has moved on completely. He's back to baseline incredulous-and-ignored — but now WE (and he)
  know he's right. The comedy is the gaslighting-by-normalcy. Mine the "wait, that happened and we're just…
  doing sports?" energy.
- **~ep15 — next bigger escalation**; the full sustained crack / extended tonal shift is later still.
