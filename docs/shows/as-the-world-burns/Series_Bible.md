## What is the MACU? (inherited context)

The **Mayor Awesome Cinematic Universe** is a deadpan, post-apocalyptic, retro-futurist world rendered as
black-and-white grainy 1970s analog television. Defining register: **the facts of the world are insane and
are stated as plain fact** — never explained, never winked at; the cast never laughs, the audience does.
Flagship: **The MACU Report** (Ron & Walter's faux evening-news show). AWB shares that dead world and bone-
dry tone, then specializes into **daytime soap opera**.

## Logline

A bone-dry daytime soap set in the MACU's dead world. Catastrophically privileged people, sealed in the
largest bunker on the shore of a lake that no longer exists, conduct the most overwrought interpersonal
melodrama imaginable over a fortune built on something profoundly stupid. The world ended decades ago.
Nobody acknowledges it. They have brunch.

**Tagline:** _"Like ash through the hourglass… these are the days of what's left of our lives."_

## The One Rule (tone law)

**Bone dry. It takes itself to death.** The facts of this world are insane and are stated, always, as plain
fact. A character delivers "The Tangerine accord collapsed in '09 and your grandfather never forgave the
Vandermeers" with total funereal gravity, and the show moves on. The cast plays it at the exact pitch of a
real 1980s soap, against a backdrop that is quietly, completely deranged. **The audience does all the
laughing.**

## Setting — Mirabelle Shores

- An exclusive lakeside community on **Lake Mirabel**, which is a **cracked clay pit**. There is no water;
  there is no clean water anywhere in this world. The residents are **not in denial** — they've genuinely
  transferred all aesthetic appreciation onto the clay. *"The clay's just gorgeous this time of year,
  Vivian. You can see the strata."* Admiring the clay is a social skill. Acknowledging the clay is the bit.
- The community is named for an amenity that no longer exists. Nobody updates the brochure. They "summer at
  the lake."
- **Seat of power:** the **largest (most luxurious) bunker at the edge of Lake Mirabel**, held by the
  Vandermeers — a sealed blast-door fortress they entertain in like a ballroom (chandeliers, crystal, a
  butler in tails). Ballroom manners inside bunker walls: a permanent visual joke.

## The economy — "the Product"

Old money (three generations) founded on something idiotic that became sacred by accident:

- A **container barge of Crystal Light** ("the Product") circled the dead globe on autopilot ~40 years
  after the bombs and ran aground in the founder's garden.
- The Vandermeers control the **last drink powder on Earth** — flavor without substance, color without
  nutrition, a luxury that is *literally empty.* Administered via the sacred **Pitcher** (there is one
  ceremonial Pitcher; mixing the Product is a rite, spilling it is regicide; at a funeral you mix a scoop
  and do **not** drink it — the waste is the point).
- Lore texture, always stated flat: the **Raspberry Lemonade reserves** (wars fought), the **Tangerine
  accord of '09** (not spoken of), flavor embargoes, rival flavor houses.

## Core premise / episode engine

A closed economy: everyone needs the Product, the Vandermeers control it, so everyone must marry in, work
for them, or scheme — and none of them can leave (out there is the world, and the world is burning). That
forces the cast to keep colliding. Weekly melodrama + a signature death gag + a cliffhanger sting.

## Signature running gag — the Resurrection Engine

Run on **ONE cursed character** (currently **Brick Vandermeer**) so it escalates; play every stage with
identical total sincerity. Full mechanic + live state tracked in **Story_Arcs.md**. Arc: "I got better" →
confirmed-dead-then-**ghost** → **ball of pure energy** → **Sheldon the turtle**. The family accepts each
downgrade as a minor social adjustment. The audience knows Sheldon is the smartest one in the bunker.

## Core cast (full detail in Character_Prompt_Bible.md)

- **Vivian Vandermeer** — the matriarch; controls the Pitcher; glacial and cruelly civil. The center.
- **Mona** — the amnesiac audience surrogate; one secret she doesn't know she has.
