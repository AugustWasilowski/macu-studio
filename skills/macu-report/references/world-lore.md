# MACU World Lore

The Mayor Awesome Cinematic Universe is a fictional, comedic, post-apocalyptic near-future. Nothing is
grounded in reality and facts can be invented freely as long as they fit the tone. The whole thing is shot
through an intentionally bad early-text-to-video aesthetic — that jank is the point, not a bug.

## Creative invariants (don't break these)

- **Everything is black-and-white, analog, and janky.** No clean/digital elements exist in this future.
- **The generation is intentionally awful** — early-AI-video wobble, low-res, grainy. It's the joke.
- **Square 1:1 framing** is part of the schtick. (A Movietone 1.19:1 crop is planned for future episodes but
  not yet standard — stay square unless August says otherwise.)
- **Tone:** earnest-anchor delivery over absurd, often creepy content. Dry, formal, a little menacing,
  comedic. Plays especially well in the HAL-9000 voice that currently voices everyone.

## Canon

- **The AI Wars of 2028** are in the past — humanity beat back a rogue-AI catastrophe. Exactly when "now" is
  stays deliberately vague.
- **Dormant AI** still lurks inside old electronics; people are warned to inspect/disassemble salvaged
  devices. (This is why a "safe, analog-only" companion like SAFE is both a comfort and a punchline.)
- **Geography:** the Capitol Remnants (ruined seat of government), the Eastern Wastelands, the Southern
  Settlements, the Western Wastes, the heartland. Travel is dangerous; weather is lethal (radiation winds,
  heat domes, acid rain).
- **Daily life:** scavenging, makeshift communities, rebuilding from the old world. Resilience-and-ruin is
  the emotional backdrop the comedy plays against.

## The show

**The MACU Report** is a community broadcast anchored by **Ron** (lead, Burgundy-esque) and **Walter**
(senior, Cronkite-esque). It runs as a newscast: cold-open intro → field segments interleaved with ads and
sponsor bumpers → sports/weather → a feel-good human-interest closer → signoff ("Good night, MACU, and good
luck."). A scrolling ticker carries absurd headlines (e.g. "MAYOR AWESOME APPROVAL RATING REACHES 147
PERCENT").

## Running gags & sponsors

- **SAFE — the Strictly Analog Friendship Engine:** a "safe" AI companion (HAL-voiced) that insists it will
  never turn on humanity, *for real this time*, while mentioning it's already removed your off switch.
- **Norm from Nuclear / the Glow Plant:** "a little glow never hurt nobody."
- **The Tally Man:** ominous PSA bumpers about who is "still counted."
- **Theme music:** soft AI big-band stings under the intro and outro (the "MACU Report theme").
- Sponsors and characters are disposable and inventable — make new ones; the universe is a sandbox.

## Production reality (affects writing)

- **Voices:** each character has its own cloned voice (via OmniVoice) — Ron sounds like Will Ferrell, Walter
  like Cronkite, the Tally Man like Attenborough, and so on. **HAL (the Piper voice) is reserved for the
  AI/appliance characters** — SAFE, the Vendor, EVERWELL — where the calm machine register is the punchline.
  Write to each character's voice; cast them per character in the manifest's `voice.speaker_map`.
- **Line text = VO + subtitle.** Keep spoken lines as clean prose; the same text is spoken and burned in as a
  subtitle (Better VCR font). Put stage directions in parentheses, not in the spoken text.
