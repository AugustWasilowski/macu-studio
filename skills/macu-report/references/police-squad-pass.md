# The Police Squad pass — deadpan non-sequiturs

A focused punch-up pass run **after the writers' room** (step 2.5) and before cast lock. One expert agent
reads the revised draft and threads in **deadpan non-sequiturs** in the Police Squad! / Airplane! / Naked Gun
(Zucker-Abrahams-Zucker) tradition: a character states something **alarming, absurd, or quietly devastating
in a completely settled register**, nobody reacts, and the scene just moves on.

It's a thin, surgical layer — it does not rewrite jokes or plot. It adds 1–2 throwaway beats per segment that
deepen the MACU's "everyone is serenely calm about the apocalypse" tone.

## The rule

> Say the unsettling thing flat. Don't acknowledge it. Cut.

- **Settled register.** The line is delivered as pleasant small talk, a status update, or a housekeeping
  note. The horror lives in the content, never the delivery.
- **No acknowledgment.** Nobody gasps, explains, or reacts (except Ron — below). The next line proceeds as if
  nothing was said.
- **Walter is the prime vehicle.** He's functionally the show's announcer, so alarming asides land hardest in
  his mouth — he reports a missing dog and a man in a fountain with the same warmth as the weather. The
  machines (STRIDE, the Vendor, SAFE) are the second-best vehicle: cheerful indifference.
- **Ron is the pressure valve.** Ron is the one character allowed to *almost* notice. His self-aware-but-not-
  aware-enough buttons ("Something is definitely wrong with me.") pay off the deadpan around him.
- **One beat, then move on.** Don't stack. A single clean non-sequitur per segment beats three.

## Techniques (named, so the pass can reach for them)

- **The eulogy/recipe collision** — one line that reads as two things at once (a recipe note *and* a
  eulogy). Funniest when it's ambiguous, never explained.
- **Validate-then-forget (machine indifference)** — a machine affirms the human, then instantly drops them
  for a metric. ("...You think so?" / "Step count: four hundred and one.")
- **The rating that's a body count** — a cheerful stat whose second half is quietly lethal ("a four-star
  rating. There are only three stars left.").
- **The warm line with a dark tail** — a friendly sentiment that curdles on the last clause ("Friends keep
  you warm. Especially in a stew.").
- **A single beat of pure normalcy** — drop one utterly mundane exchange into the chaos right before a
  payoff, so the reaction lands harder ("Roscoe, baby. Inside voice." / "My apologies, Doctor.").
- **The self-aware button (Ron)** — Ron states the wrongness plainly, one beat too late to act on it.

## Keep it pipeline-clean

Same authoring rules as the rest of the script: the spoken line is **clean prose** (it becomes the VO *and*
the burned subtitle); put delivery notes in parentheses on their own line, never inside the spoken text.
Protect the weird — if a beat is confusing rather than strange, cut it; never sand off the strangeness.

## Canonical worked examples (the reference set)

These are the model — match this voice and restraint. (From the "Welcome to the Practice" pass.)

**Cold open — after Walter's Glowboys line:**
> WALTER: And I'm Walter. A lovely, settled evening. The Glowboys have adjusted beautifully — three of them
> are directing traffic, one's been standing in the fountain since Tuesday. Part of the family now. (beat)
> The settlement's dog has also gone missing. Unrelated.

**Segment 1 — after STRIDE's step count:**
> STRIDE: (instantly, bright) Step count: thirty-two! You're doing great, Ron!
> RON: (quietly, to himself) It counted me sighing. That was a sigh.

**Segment 2 — after Rose's "Love. And Earl's cousin.":**
> RON: (laughing, charmed) Ha — the recipe. You'll never give it up. Cooks.
> COACH BODHI: (eyes still closed) Earl's cousin was a very giving man. Double portions. God rest.

— reads as a eulogy and a recipe note simultaneously.

**Segment 2 — after STRIDE's "place setting" icon appears:**
> STRIDE: (bright) Objective updated, Ron! The feast needs guests, and you are wonderful with people.
> RON: (genuinely touched) ...You think so?
> STRIDE: Step count: four hundred and one.

— it validated him and immediately forgot him.

**Segment 3 — Walter's intro to Goldtooth:**
> WALTER: ...the wasteland's premier dentist: Dr. Goldtooth. He has a four-star rating. There are only three
> stars left.

**Segment 3 — after Roscoe's aria, before Ron's "...Jethuth":**
> DR. GOLDTOOTH: (not looking up, working) Roscoe, baby. Inside voice.
> ROSCOE: (instant calm) My apologies, Doctor.

— one beat of pure normalcy amid the chaos before Ron's frozen reaction makes the "...Jethuth" land harder.

**Weather — Walter's closer:**
> WALTER: Lovely weather for a gathering. (beat) Bring layers. And a friend. Friends keep you warm.
> Especially in a stew.

— he doesn't know what he's saying. He never does.

**Signoff — Ron's homework speech:**
> RON: ...bring a friend, bring a neighbor, bring everyone you can find to Rose's feast Friday. The more the
> merrier. (beat) I've never felt this good. I want you to know that. (another beat) Something is definitely
> wrong with me.

— self-aware but not quite aware enough. Very Ron.

## How to run the pass

Dispatch it like the writers' room — a focused expert subagent (or inline if light): hand it the revised
script + this file and have it return **only proposed insertions** — a segment anchor plus the new line(s) —
so August can accept/reject each. Default to Walter and the machines; cap at ~1–2 beats per segment; never
break the plot or step on an existing punchline. Apply the accepted beats, then continue to the cast lock and
the manifest.
